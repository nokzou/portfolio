Innovation. That's the future of our world. That's what I'd love to do. Creating things and watching them work has always been my passion, and that's what I'd love to work at as a career. People often take one way to fix a problem , but I believe that there's always multiple methods for one goal. I do this to keep an open mind, something a lot of us struggle with these days. An open mind and a positive attitude are all that I need in life. It's not easy to innovate, though. It takes a special person to come up with new, creative ideas, and often even they fail. If I want to pursue innovation in my career, then I'll have to gain experience and face failure over and over, and that's what I'm perfectly ready to do.


I'm truly grateful to have been born in my generation. I grew up never knowing what it was like to not have computers, which led to their importance in my childhood. Around the age of seven, I built my first PC with my dad using old, leftover parts and some new, cheap ones from the nearby Frys. I failed over and over. but the moment that I succeeded was the sweetest victory of my life. Spending time and work on a creation and being able to watch it work is something truly indescribable. It's an amazing thing when a person is perseverant, and that's what I strive to be. The first thing I ever did with that computer was play video games and marvel at the idea that the project that I had just finished was able to do something so complex. I've built two more computers (for myself) ever since, but I never really stuck to the hardware side of things. I always found the software to be far more interesting. I spend a large amount of my time playing video games, exploring the creations of others, and oftentimes I wish I was the creator, and that's what computer science can allow me to be.
